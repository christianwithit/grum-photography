Get To Know Me

I'm Grum. I photograph weddings and portraits across Uganda.

The Work, and How I Got Here

A Passion for Authentic Moments

Photography came before weddings for me — but once I shot my first ceremony, that became the focus. I've since documented weddings across Uganda: Kampala, Jinja, Lake Victoria, the Rwenzori foothills. The geography changes; the approach doesn't. I work to make images that hold the actual feeling of the day — not a polished version of it.

How I Work

Every Wedding is a
Unique Story

My approach isn't one thing or the other — not purely documentary, not purely directed. I read the room. For most of the day, I'm in the background; when you need a nudge or a better angle, I'll say so. The goal is coverage that's complete: the ceremony, the in-between moments, the details that don't make it into the speech but matter just as much.

The Process

What To Expect

01

Inquiry

Reach out with your date and a bit about what you're planning. I'll send over package details and answer your questions. If it's a good fit, we'll set up a call.

02

Booking

A signed agreement and retainer holds your date. From there, I'll send over resources to help you plan the photo side of things and we'll map out a realistic timeline.

03

The Day

On the day, I'm there to document — not to produce. Candid coverage is the default; direction comes in when it's useful. You won't feel managed.
